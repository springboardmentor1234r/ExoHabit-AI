What Are Exoplanets?

Planets that orbit stars beyond our Sun

üåç

Definition

An exoplanet is any planet beyond our solar system. Most orbit other stars, but free-floating exoplanets, called rogue planets, orbit the galactic center and are untethered to any star.

üî≠

Discovery Methods

Scientists use various methods: Transit method (detecting dimming of starlight), Radial velocity (measuring star wobble), Direct imaging, Microlensing, and Astrometry.

üå°Ô∏è

Habitable Zone

The "Goldilocks Zone" is the region around a star where conditions are just right for liquid water to exist on a planet's surface - neither too hot nor too cold.

üöÄ

Kepler & TESS

NASA's Kepler and TESS missions have discovered thousands of exoplanets by staring at patches of sky and measuring the brightness of thousands of stars.

Famous Exoplanets

Meet the celebrities of the exoplanet world

Proxima Centauri b

Closest Exoplanet

Our nearest neighbor, just 4.2 light-years away. Located in the habitable zone of a red dwarf star, this rocky world has captured humanity's imagination as our first potential interstellar destination.

Distance 4.2 light-years
Orbital Period 11.2 days
Mass 1.17 Earth masses

TRAPPIST-1e

Seven Earths

One of seven Earth-sized planets in the TRAPPIST-1 system. Located 40 light-years away, it's considered one of the most potentially habitable exoplanets discovered.

Distance 40 light-years
Radius 0.92 Earth radii
Temperature 251 K

Kepler-452b

Earth's Cousin

Often called "Earth's older cousin," this super-Earth orbits a sun-like star in the habitable zone. It's about 1.6 times Earth's radius and 60% larger in mass.

Distance 1,400 light-years
Orbital Period 385 days
Age 6 billion years

WASP-12b

The Doom Planet

A "hot Jupiter" being consumed by its star. Tidally locked and stretched into an egg shape, this dark planet absorbs 94% of light, making it pitch black with a temperature of 2,210¬∞C.

Distance 870 light-years
Temperature 2,210¬∞C
Status Being destroyed

K2-18b

Water World

A super-Earth with water vapor detected in its atmosphere. Located 124 light-years away, it's 2.6 times Earth's radius and could potentially have a water ocean.

Distance 124 light-years
Mass 8.6 Earth masses
Atmosphere Water vapor detected

SETI & The Search Continues

The Search for Extraterrestrial Intelligence (SETI) listens for radio signals from advanced civilizations. Projects like Breakthrough Listen are scanning millions of stars for artificial signals.

The Drake Equation

N = R* √ó fp √ó ne √ó fl √ó fi √ó fc √ó L

Estimates the number of active, communicative extraterrestrial civilizations in the Milky Way galaxy.

R* = Rate of star formation
fp = Fraction with planets
ne = Planets that can support life
fl = Fraction where life develops
fi = Fraction with intelligent life
fc = Fraction that communicate
L = Length of time they release detectable signals

The Fermi Paradox

"Where is everybody?" - Enrico Fermi, 1950

Given the billions of stars and high probability of habitable planets, why haven't we found any signs of extraterrestrial civilizations? Possible solutions include:

  • We're alone (Rare Earth hypothesis)
  • Civilizations destroy themselves before achieving interstellar travel
  • They exist but choose not to communicate
  • They communicate in ways we don't understand
  • We're looking in the wrong places or at the wrong times
